Economic Indicators & Venture Capital Funding in Nigeria (2015–2023)

Investigated how macroeconomic variables — GDP, inflation, and exchange rates — influence venture capital investment in Nigeria's startup ecosystem over an 8-year period.

  • GDP growth showed strong positive correlation with VC inflows (r = 0.74)
  • Inflation above 18% triggered 30–45% drops in investment
  • Exchange rate stability was the #1 predictor of investor confidence
Policy Impact: Brief submitted recommending CBN monetary tightening strategies

Gender Gap in Financial Inclusion — Northern Nigeria

Assessed structural and cultural barriers preventing women across Kano, Kaduna, and Sokoto from accessing formal financial services. Sample: 350 respondents.

  • 41% cited lack of ID documents as primary barrier
  • Cultural norms blocked access for 35% of respondents
  • Mobile money identified as the highest-potential bridging solution
Policy Impact: Formal policy brief submitted to Kano State Ministry of Finance

Faster recovery for digital businesses

Post-COVID SME Resilience in Nigeria's Informal Sector

Examined how Nigeria's informal SMEs survived, adapted, and recovered from COVID-19 economic shocks — 200 surveys and 20 in-depth interviews.

  • 68% of SMEs lost more than 50% of revenue during lockdowns
  • Digital payment adoption was the single strongest recovery predictor
  • Only 12% of eligible SMEs received any government support
Policy Impact: Working paper with digital onboarding recommendations
